# Chunk 02 - COBIT 2019

COBIT 2019 defines 40 governance and management objectives in 5 domains plus 7 information criteria, 11 design factors, and focus area guidance.

## EDM: Evaluate, Direct and Monitor (5)

| ID | Objective | Assessment Focus |
|----|-----------|-----------------|
| EDM01 | Ensured governance setting and maintenance | Governance framework evaluated and improved |
| EDM02 | Ensured benefits delivery | Programs deliver benefits on time, within budget |
| EDM03 | Ensured risk optimization | IT risk optimized within risk appetite |
| EDM04 | Ensured resource optimization | Resources optimized and sustainable |
| EDM05 | Ensured stakeholder transparency | Stakeholders informed of performance, risks, decisions |

## 7 Information Criteria

Effectiveness, Efficiency, Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability, Compliance, Reliability.

## 11 Design Factors

1. Enterprise Strategy
2. Enterprise Goals
3. Risk Profile
4. I&T-Related Issues
5. Threat Landscape
6. Compliance Requirements
7. Role of IT
8. Sourcing Model for IT
9. IT Implementation Methods
10. Technology Adoption Strategy
11. Enterprise Size

## Focus Areas

COBIT 2019 provides governance and management objectives as the core model. Specific Focus Area guidance may be published separately by ISACA. The ISACA COBIT 2019 Design Guide describes how to apply design factors to create tailored governance systems for specific contexts.

> **Disclaimer:** ISACA publishes Focus Area guides (e.g., for small and medium enterprises, information security, DevOps, risk management). These are the official ISACA COBIT 2019 Focus Area publications.

## Assessment Procedure (10 Steps)

1. Select objectives. 2. Map goals cascade. 3. Determine current capability (0-5 CMMI). 4. Determine target capability. 5. Identify gaps. 6. Develop design factors. 7. Create tailored governance system. 8. Implement. 9. Monitor. 10. Assess maturity improvement. Cycle: Steps 1-7 design phase, 8-10 implementation. Loop back to Step 3.

## Decision logic

- Use EDM for governance/board questions. Use APO for planning/strategy. Use BAI for build/change. Use DSS for operations. Use MEA for performance/compliance.
- Cross-cutting: apply design factors for tailoring, information criteria for evaluation.
