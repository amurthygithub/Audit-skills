# Chunk 02 - COBIT 2019

COBIT 2019 defines **40 governance and management objectives in 5 domains** (5 EDM + 14 APO + 11 BAI + 6 DSS + 4 MEA), 11 design factors, the goals cascade, and focus area guidance. Objective names below are verbatim from the COBIT 2019 Framework: Governance and Management Objectives publication (verified 2026-06-10).

| ID | Objective | Assessment Focus |
|----|-----------|-----------------|
| EDM01 | Ensured Governance Framework Setting and Maintenance | Governance framework evaluated and improved |
| EDM02 | Ensured Benefits Delivery | Programs deliver benefits on time, within budget |
| EDM03 | Ensured Risk Optimization | IT risk optimized within risk appetite |
| EDM04 | Ensured Resource Optimization | Resources optimized and sustainable |
| EDM05 | Ensured Stakeholder Engagement | Stakeholders engaged; informed of performance, risks, decisions |

## DSS: Deliver, Service and Support (6)

| ID | Objective | Assessment Focus |
|----|-----------|-----------------|
| DSS01 | Managed Operations | Operations per procedures, service levels |
| DSS02 | Managed Service Requests and Incidents | Requests fulfilled, incidents resolved |
| DSS03 | Managed Problems | Root cause analysis, permanent fixes |

Key DSS05 management practices (verbatim): DSS05.02 "Manage network and connectivity security", DSS05.04 "Manage user identity and logical access", DSS05.07 "Manage vulnerabilities and monitor the infrastructure for security-related events". ## Goals Cascade (replaces the COBIT 4.1 "information criteria")

COBIT 2019 prioritizes objectives through the **goals cascade**: stakeholder needs -> enterprise goals -> alignment goals -> governance/management objectives. The seven "information criteria" (effectiveness, efficiency, confidentiality, integrity, availability, compliance, reliability) are a **COBIT 4.1 construct that does not exist in COBIT 2019**. Where this skill's output templates tag findings with `cobit_criteria_affected` (e.g., Confidentiality/Integrity/Compliance), treat those as information-attribute tags -- a house convention for readability -- not COBIT 2019 framework elements.

## 11 Design Factors

1. Enterprise Strategy
2. Enterprise Goals
3. Risk Profile
4. I&T-Related Issues
5. Threat Landscape
6. Compliance Requirements
7. Role of IT
8. Sourcing Model for IT
9. IT Implementation Methods
10. Technology Adoption Strategy
11. Enterprise Size

The COBIT 2019 Design Guide describes how to apply the design factors to tailor a governance system; it does not prescribe numeric weights (any weighting in this skill's worked examples is an illustrative heuristic).

## Focus Areas

Official ISACA COBIT focus-area publications (per isaca.org/resources/cobit, verified 2026-06-10): **COBIT Focus Area: Information Security**, **COBIT Focus Area: Information and Technology Risk**, **COBIT Focus Area: DevOps Using COBIT 2019**, and **COBIT 2019 for Small and Medium Enterprises**. ISACA also publishes *Implementing the NIST Cybersecurity Framework Using COBIT 2019* (maps CSF v1.1, not CSF 2.0). Do not cite focus-area titles beyond these without verifying at ISACA.org.

## Assessment Procedure (10 Steps)

1. Select objectives. 2. Map goals cascade. 3. Determine current capability (0-5 CMMI-based CPM). 4. Determine target capability. 5. Identify gaps. 6. Develop design factors. 7. Create tailored governance system. 8. Implement. 9. Monitor. 10. Assess capability improvement. Cycle: Steps 1-7 design phase, 8-10 implementation. Loop back to Step 3.

## Decision logic

- Use EDM for governance/board questions. Use APO for planning/strategy. Use BAI for build/change. Use DSS for operations. Use MEA for performance/compliance/assurance (MEA04 for audit engagements).
- Cross-cutting: apply design factors for tailoring; use the goals cascade for prioritization.
