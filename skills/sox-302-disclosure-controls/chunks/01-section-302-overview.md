# Chunk 01 — Section 302 Overview

SOX §302 — "Corporate responsibility for financial reports," codified at **15 U.S.C. 7241** — directed the SEC to require, by rule, a certification by each issuer's signing officers in every annual and quarterly report filed under the Securities Exchange Act [SOX-302-Statute-15USC7241 §a]. The SEC implemented it primarily through **17 CFR 240.13a-14** (who signs, filed as an exhibit) and **17 CFR 240.13a-15** (the DC&P/ICFR definitions and the evaluation requirement). The certification text consumers sign is the **Regulation S-K Item 601(b)(31)** exhibit, whose 6 numbered paragraphs mirror the 6 statutory elements.

## 1. What §302 requires

The statute requires the signing officers to certify **6 elements** in each periodic report [SOX-302-Statute-15USC7241 §a]:

1. the signing officer has reviewed the report;
2. based on the officer's knowledge, the report contains no untrue statement of a material fact and omits no material fact necessary to make the statements not misleading;
3. based on knowledge, the financial statements and other financial information fairly present in all material respects the financial condition and results of operations;
4. the officers are responsible for establishing and maintaining DC&P (and ICFR), have designed them, and have evaluated and reported on DC&P effectiveness;
5. the officers have disclosed to the auditors and the audit committee all significant deficiencies and material weaknesses in ICFR, and any fraud (material or not) involving management or other employees with a significant role in ICFR;
6. the officers have indicated any significant ICFR changes.

The 6 elements are unpacked in `chunks/03-the-six-certifications.md`.

## 2. Who signs — two officers (§240.13a-14)

Each report on Form 10-Q, 10-K, 20-F, or 40-F "must include certifications in the form specified in the applicable exhibit filing requirements … and such certifications must be filed as an **exhibit** to such report" [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §a]. The **principal executive officer and principal financial officer** of the issuer (or persons performing similar functions) at the time of filing must each sign a certification — **2 signing officers** (PEO + PFO) [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §a].

- A signer **may not** delegate the certification: it may not be signed "on his or her behalf pursuant to a power of attorney or other form of confirming authority" [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §c]. The officers sign personally.
- During a newly-public transition, the officers may omit the part of paragraph 4 introductory language and paragraph 4(b) that refers to designing and maintaining **ICFR** until the issuer becomes subject to the §240.13a-15 / §240.15d-15 ICFR requirements [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §a]. The DC&P portion of the certification is not omitted — see `chunks/05-section-302-vs-404.md`.

## 3. Cadence — quarterly and annual

The §302 certification accompanies **each** annual and quarterly periodic report. The underlying **DC&P evaluation** that supports paragraph 4 is performed by management "as of the end of **each fiscal quarter**," except a foreign private issuer (FPI) evaluates "as of the end of each fiscal year" [CFR-17-240.13a-15 §b]. So a domestic issuer certifies (and evaluates DC&P) four times a year; an FPI evaluates annually. See `chunks/04-evaluation-and-disclosure.md`.

## 4. The §906 companion certification (pointer only)

A second certification — under **Section 1350 of Title 18** (18 U.S.C. 1350, enacted by SOX §906) — must accompany each periodic report containing financial statements and is **furnished** (not filed) as an exhibit; it may be satisfied by a single certification signed by the PEO and PFO [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §b]. The §906 certification is the **criminal** certification; it is the companion to the §302 certification. This skill names it and does not detail it — the §302 certification is the operative artifact here.

## 6. Anti-hallucination

- There are **2** signing officers (PEO + PFO), each signing personally; a power of attorney may not sign [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §c].
- The §302 certification is **filed** as an exhibit; the §906 certification is **furnished** as an exhibit [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §a] [CFR-17-240.13a-14 §b]. Do not conflate the two.
- §302 applies to **each** periodic report, quarterly and annual; the supporting DC&P evaluation is quarterly for domestic issuers, annual for FPIs [CFR-17-240.13a-15 §b].
