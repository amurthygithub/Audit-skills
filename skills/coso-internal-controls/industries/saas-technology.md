# Industry: SaaS / Technology (Pre-IPO SOX 404 Readiness)

## 1. Posture

Pre-IPO SaaS companies preparing for SOX 404 compliance face unique challenges. The JOBs Act provides a transition period (up to 5 years as an EGC) during which SOX 404(b) auditor attestation is not required, but SOX 404(a) management assessment remains mandatory.

| Stage | SOX requirement | COSO focus |
|-------|---------------|------------|
| Pre-IPO (private) | None | Build RcM, walkthroughs, entity-level controls — readiness posture |
| IPO year | SOX 404(a) management assessment | First management ICFR report at fiscal year-end |
| EGC (year 1-5) | SOX 404(a) only | COSO 2013 ICIF; no 404(b) auditor attestation |
| Post-EGC (accelerated filer) | SOX 404(a) + 404(b) | Full integrated audit per PCAOB AS 2201 |

## 2. Entity-Level Control Profile

- **P1 (Integrity and ethics)**: Start-up culture often prioritizes speed over process; tone at the top and code of conduct must mature.
- **P4 (Competence)**: Rapid hiring may outpace competence frameworks; recruitment and training processes must scale.
- **P11 (Technology controls)**: SaaS companies are technology-native but may lack formal ITGCs (access reviews, change management, SDLC). This is the most common deficiency area.
- **P16-P17 (Monitoring)**: Monitoring is typically the last component to mature; continuous monitoring and internal audit may not exist pre-IPO.

## 3. Deficiency Classification Impact

- **ITGC deficiencies**: The most common deficiency area. A single ineffective ITGC (e.g., no access review) can cascade into multiple process-level deficiencies, triggering the aggregation rule.
- **Emerging tech (RPA, GenAI)**: SaaS companies are early adopters of automation and AI. See COSO RPA Guidance (2024) and GenAI Guidance (2026) in chunk 07.
- **Compensating controls**: Application-level automated controls (edit checks, validations) may compensate for ITGC deficiencies if precise enough.

## 5. Pain Points

- ITGC maturity — SaaS companies often have informal IT processes; formalizing ITGCs (access management, change management, IT operations) is the biggest readiness gap.
- Revenue recognition — ASC 606 is complex for SaaS (performance obligations, variable consideration, contract modifications); revenue walkthroughs are critical.
- Key control documentation — first-year SOX teams tend to document too many controls; focus on key controls that address relevant assertions.
- Stock-based compensation — equity administration controls become material post-IPO.
- Sub-processor / vendor risk — SaaS depends on cloud providers and sub-processors; evaluate whether vendor SOC reports provide evidence for your ICFR.
