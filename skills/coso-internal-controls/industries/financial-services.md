# Industry: Financial Services (public-company SOX 404)

## 1. Posture

Public-company financial services entities (banks, insurance, capital markets, payments, fintech) are subject to SOX 404(a) and SOX 404(b) if accelerated or large accelerated filers. COSO 2013 ICIF is the de facto ICFR framework.

| Sub-sector | Filing status | SOX 404(b) | COSO focus |
|-----------|--------------|------------|------------|
| Large bank (SIFI) | Large accelerated | Required | Full 17-principle assessment; MWs must be disclosed |
| Regional bank | Accelerated | Required | Scoped ICFR; significant accounts driven by loan portfolio and deposit operations |
| Insurance carrier | Large accelerated or accelerated | Usually required | Policy reserves, reinsurance, premium recognition |
| Capital markets / broker-dealer | Accelerated | Required | Trading controls, segregation of customer assets, trade settlement |
| Payments / fintech (public) | Accelerated | Required | Transaction processing, PCI-DSS overlay, fraud controls |
| Pre-IPO fintech | Pre-IPO | Not yet required | SOX 404 readiness — build RcM, walkthroughs, entity-level controls now |

## 2. Entity-Level Control Profile

- **P2 (Board oversight)**: Financial services audit committees are highly scrutinized. Director expertise and independence are regulatory expectations (OCC, FDIC, FRB).
- **P5 (Accountability)**: Compensation structures (bonuses, equity) create incentive pressure; evaluate excessive pressure PoF carefully.
- **P8 (Fraud risk)**: Financial services has elevated fraud risk (customer funds, transaction volume, complex instruments). Fraud risk assessment must be comprehensive.

## 3. Deficiency Classification Impact

- **Regulatory overlay**: A material weakness may trigger additional regulatory scrutiny beyond SEC disclosure (OCC enforcement action, FDIC consent order, FRB heightened supervision).
- **Aggregation risk**: Financial services often has many small controls across many processes; the aggregation rule (AS 2201.68) is critical.
- **Compensating controls**: Reconciliation controls (bank rec, custody rec, intercompany) are frequently precise enough to compensate for process-level deficiencies.

## 5. Pain Points

- SOX 404(b) scope creep — auditor testing of non-key controls; challenge the auditor on key vs. non-key.
- Regulatory expectation gap — OCC/FDIC may require controls beyond SOX 404; map these to COSO.
- Compensating control over-reliance — reconciliation controls must be tested for operating effectiveness, not just design.
- Multi-location considerations (AS 2201.B1-B38) — regional banks with many branches; shared services center controls.
