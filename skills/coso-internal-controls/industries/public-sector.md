# Industry: Public Sector (Federal and State Government)

## 1. Posture

Public-sector entities (federal agencies, state governments, special-purpose governments) generally are not subject to SOX 404. However, SOX-like requirements exist:

| Entity | Framework | COSO application |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Federal CFO Act agency | OMB Circular A-123, Appendix A | A-123 (per M-16-17) requires GAO's Green Book (GAO-14-704G) — which adapts COSO's 17 principles for government — as the internal-control framework; ICFR-like assessment required |
| State government | Single Audit (2 CFR Part 200) | Internal control over compliance per COSO framework |
| Government corporation (e.g., GSE) | SOX 404 applies if publicly traded | Full COSO 2013 ICIF |
| Public pension fund | GASB 67/68 | Actuarial controls; COSO applied to investment valuation and benefit payments |

## 2. Entity-Level Control Profile

- **P2 (Board oversight)**: Public-sector boards and audit committees often include political appointees; independence assessment must consider political relationships.
- **P3 (Structure/reporting)**: Organizational structures are statutory, not flexible; assess whether the mandated structure enables internal control.
- **P15 (External communication)**: Public-sector external communication includes legislative bodies, oversight agencies, inspectors general.

## 3. Deficiency Classification Impact

- **OMB A-123**: Material weaknesses in federal financial reporting must be reported to OMB, Congress, and GAO.
- **Single Audit findings**: Classified as material weakness or significant deficiency per OMB guidance.
- **Public accountability**: Deficiency communication is broader — legislative bodies, the public, ratings agencies.

## 4. Top Use Cases

- OMB A-123 internal control assessment (planned UC-03)
- Single Audit internal control over compliance

## 5. Pain Points

- Framework adaptation — COSO was designed for private-sector entities; adapt to public-sector governance and accountability structures.
- Legislative oversight — material weaknesses may trigger congressional hearings, not just financial disclosures.
- Budget control integration — public-sector financial reporting controls are intertwined with budgetary compliance controls.
