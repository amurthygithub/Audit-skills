# Chunk 03 — SOX 404 and PCAOB AS 2201

## SOX 404(a) — Management Assessment

Management MUST:
1. Establish and maintain adequate ICFR.
2. Assess effectiveness of ICFR as of fiscal year-end.
3. Identify the framework used (COSO 2013 ICIF is the de facto standard).
4. Include assessment in the annual report (Form 10-K for SEC registrants).
5. Disclose any material weaknesses identified.
6. State that the registered public accounting firm has issued an attestation report.

## SOX 404(b) — Auditor Attestation

The auditor MUST:
1. Attest to, and report on, management's assessment of ICFR.
2. Not perform a separate engagement — the ICFR attestation must be integrated with the financial statement audit.
3. Express an opinion on whether the company maintained effective ICFR in all material respects.
4. Conduct the integrated audit per PCAOB AS 2201.

## SOX 302 — Disclosure Controls Certification

- CEO and CFO must certify the design and effectiveness of disclosure controls.
- Disclosure controls encompass ICFR but are broader (include non-financial information).
- Deficiencies in ICFR may also affect disclosure controls certification.

## JOBS Act Exemptions

- **EGCs**: Exempt from SOX 404(b) auditor attestation for up to 5 years after IPO.
- **Smaller Reporting Companies**: Exempt from SOX 404(b).
- **Non-Accelerated Filers**: Exempt from SOX 404(b).
- All companies remain subject to SOX 404(a) management assessment.

## PCAOB AS 2201 — Top-Down Approach

### Planning (AS 2201.09-.20)

1. Evaluate entity risk — size, complexity, organizational structure, risk of misstatement.
2. Address fraud risk — incorporate fraud risk factors.
3. Use the work of others — evaluate competence and objectivity.
4. Determine materiality — same as financial statement audit.
5. Scale the audit per AS 2201.13.

### Step 1 — Start at Financial Statement Level (AS 2201.21)

### Step 2 — Entity-Level Controls (AS 2201.22-.27)

Classify each ELC by precision:
- Indirect effect on ICFR: pervasive but indirect; do NOT reduce process-level testing.
- Operating as monitoring controls: monitor other controls; may reduce but not eliminate testing.
- Precise enough to prevent/detect misstatements: MAY eliminate additional process-level testing.

Period-end financial reporting controls (AS 2201.26-.27): procedures for GL entries, accounting policy selection, journal entries, adjustments, financial statement preparation.

### Step 3 — Significant Accounts and Disclosures (AS 2201.28-.33)

An account/disclosure is significant if there is a reasonable possibility of containing a misstatement that would cause financial statements to be materially misstated. Consider size, composition, susceptibility, volume, complexity, nature.

### Step 4 — Relevant Assertions

Five assertions: Existence/Occurrence, Completeness, Valuation/Allocation, Rights and Obligations, Presentation and Disclosure.

### Step 5 — Understanding Likely Sources of Misstatement (AS 2201.34-.38)

Accomplished through walkthroughs.

### Step 6 — Select Controls to Test (AS 2201.39-.41)

Test only key controls — those addressing the assessed risk of misstatement to each relevant assertion.

### Testing Design Effectiveness (AS 2201.42-.43)

Does the control, if operated as prescribed, satisfy control objectives? Methods: inquiry, inspection, walkthrough, observation.

### Testing Operating Effectiveness (AS 2201.44-.61)

- Nature: Inquiry alone is NEVER sufficient.
- Timing: Test over the period being audited; consider interim testing and roll-forward.
- Extent: Sufficient samples based on control frequency.
- Use the work of others: Evaluate competence and objectivity.

### Forming an Opinion (AS 2201.71-.74)

Based on all evidence from design/operating testing, walkthroughs, deficiency evaluation, and management's assessment.

### ICFR Assessment Workflow

Six phases:
1. Scoping and Planning — significant accounts, relevant assertions, ELC evaluation.
2. Entity-Level Assessment — Control Environment P1-P5, multi-location considerations.
3. Process-Level Assessment and Walkthroughs — I/O/I/R at each point, RcM, design effectiveness.
4. Deficiency Evaluation — decision tree, compensating controls, aggregation rule (AS 2201.68).
5. Reporting — Management's ICFR Report, Auditor's ICFR Report, written communications.
6. Ongoing Monitoring — monitoring controls, subsequent-year scaling.

### Aggregation Rule (AS 2201.68)

Assess each deficiency individually per the decision tree. Then assess whether the combined effect of deficiencies affecting the same account/assertion creates a reasonable possibility of material misstatement. If so, classify as material weakness.

### Subsequent-Year Considerations

- Leverage prior-year work: update risk assessment, reassess scoping.
- Prior-year effective controls: may reduce nature/timing but NOT eliminate testing.
- New or changed controls: first-year level testing.
- Roll-forward procedures: additional testing for period between interim test and year-end.
