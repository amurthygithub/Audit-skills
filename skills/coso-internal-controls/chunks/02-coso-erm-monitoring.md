## Framework Selection Guidance

- **ICIF 2013**: Use for ICFR assessment (SOX 404, financial reporting controls).
- **ERM 2017**: Use for enterprise risk management, strategy-risk integration.
- Both may be used together when the entity needs ICFR compliance and ERM maturity.

## COSO 2009 Monitoring Controls Guidance

### Two Fundamental Principles

**MP1**: Ongoing and/or separate evaluations enable management to determine if other components continue to function over time.
**MP2**: Internal control deficiencies are identified and communicated in a timely manner.

### Three Elements of Monitoring

**Element 1 — Establish a Basis for Monitoring**:
- Appropriate tone at the top supporting monitoring.
- Effective organizational structure assigning monitoring roles with appropriate capacity, objectivity, authority.
- Starting point or baseline of known effective internal control — if no baseline exists, the entity cannot rely on monitoring alone; separate evaluations are required.

**Element 2 — Design and Execute Monitoring Procedures**:
- Focus on persuasive information about operation of key controls addressing significant risks.
- Classify monitoring as ongoing (built into normal operations) or separate evaluations (periodic, specific reviews).
- Evaluate whether the mix is sufficient given rate of change and complexity.

**Element 3 — Assess and Report Results**:
- Assess severity of identified deficiencies using the decision tree.
- Report results to appropriate staff and board.
- Ensure timely corrective action and follow-up.

### Monitoring as Audit Evidence (AS 2201.24-.25)

Monitoring results may be used as audit evidence when procedures are rigorous, individuals are competent/objective, frequency is sufficient, and the monitoring addresses the same risks. However, monitoring controls alone CANNOT eliminate the need to test the controls being monitored.
