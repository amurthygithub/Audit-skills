# Industry: Healthcare (HIPAA-regulated, clinical revenue)

## Posture
Healthcare organizations subject to HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules, with complex clinical revenue cycles. Audit workpapers must accommodate PHI-sensitive evidence, BAA chain documentation, and sampling across disparate clinical and billing systems. Often dual-framework: financial statement audit (PCAOB/AICPA) plus HIPAA compliance attestation.

## Workpaper Infrastructure Priorities
1. Establish permanent file with BAA inventory and subservice organization mapping (billing vendors, clearinghouses, EHR hosts).
2. Design sampling methodology for clinical revenue: patient encounters, charge capture, coding accuracy, claims adjudication.
3. Document HIPAA-compliant evidence retention: PHI redaction procedures, encryption at rest, access logging per 45 CFR 164.312.
4. Create IPE validation procedures for clinical system reports (EHR extracts, billing system exports, claims scrubbing logs).
5. Build internal control documentation (C-series) covering COSO 5 components plus HIPAA-specific administrative, physical, and technical safeguards.

## Typical Sampling
- Attribute sampling for charge capture controls (ICD-10/CPT coding accuracy).
- MUS for accounts receivable (patient + third-party payor).
- Discovery sampling for suspected fraudulent billing patterns (AICPA Audit Guide on Sampling, Chapter 4).
- Variables sampling for revenue cycle estimation (net patient service revenue).
- Dual-purpose sampling where control reliance and substantive testing overlap (e.g., claims adjudication testing).

## HIPAA Evidence Retention Requirements
- PHI must be stored encrypted per 45 CFR 164.312(a)(2)(iv).
- Access logs maintained per 45 CFR 164.312(b).
- Minimum 6-year retention from creation or last effective date (45 CFR 164.530(j)(2)).
- BAA chain must be documented from covered entity through all subservice organizations handling PHI.

## Pain Points
- Clinical revenue cycle complexity (charge capture, coding, billing, collections spans multiple systems).
- PHI exposure risk in workpaper evidence; special handling for sampling selections.
- Third-party payor agreements create multi-faceted revenue recognition challenges.
- EHR system access for audit testing requires IT coordination and HIPAA-compliant access.
- Sampling clinical transactions requires understanding of DRG/APC coding and reimbursement methodologies.

## Top Use Cases
- UC-01: MUS sampling for patient AR
- UC-02: 5-part finding for charge capture control gap
