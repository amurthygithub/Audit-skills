# UC-03 — Enterprise fin-svcs maps SOC 2 → 800-53 Moderate

## Scenario

A B2B SaaS for commercial banks (FinPay Treasury Cloud) is preparing a federal agency pilot. The federal customer requires an 800-53 Moderate mapping. The SaaS has a SOC 2 Type II report and a PCI DSS v4.0 attestation. The engagement produces a crosswalk, a gap register, and a remediation plan.

**What this UC ships and what it doesn't:** the shipped crosswalk is a **curated 33-row sample** covering 39 unique 800-53 control IDs — a worked demonstration of the method, NOT the complete TSC→800-53 mapping. Because it is a sample, **no overlap percentage is derivable or claimed** (an earlier version of this UC asserted "231 mapped / 71% overlap / 94 gaps" that the shipped data could not produce — retired under SOX-637). For a real engagement, source the complete mapping from the AICPA TSC mapping resources and the NIST informative-reference materials, then run the same computation.

## Walk-through

### Step 1 — Categorize (chunks/02-categorize.md §Procedure)

The SaaS processes financial PII and wires/ACH for ~1.5M accounts. Categorization:

- **C: MODERATE** — financial PII, wire details, account numbers.
- **I: MODERATE** — wire instructions must be integrity-protected.
- **A: MODERATE** — wire transfer windows are time-sensitive.

**System security category: MODERATE.**

### Step 2 — Baseline selection (Skill §4.2)

Baseline: **Moderate** (800-53 Rev 5). Control counts come from SP 800-53B [NIST-SP-800-53B] (or the FedRAMP baseline workbook for FedRAMP contexts) — do not assert a count without checking the current publication.

### Step 3 — Crosswalk SOC 2 → 800-53 (chunks/09-crosswalk.md §Procedure)

`data/crosswalks/soc2-to-800-53-mod.json` maps representative SOC 2 Common Criteria to 800-53 controls with a strength rating (exact / partial / contextual). Computed summary of the shipped sample:

| Metric | Value (computed from the file) |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| SOC 2 Common Criteria series | 9 (CC1–CC9) |
| Sample mappings | 33 |
| Unique 800-53 control IDs covered | 39 (enhancements counted separately) |
| Overlap % | **not derivable from a sample — not emitted** |

Where SOC 2 typically adds the least 800-53 coverage (the gap concentrations): Rev 5 privacy (PT), supply chain (SR), program management (PM), and operational depth (specific AC-2/SI-4/IR-4 enhancements).

### Step 4 — Gap register and dispositions (Skill §6.2)

`data/seeds/uc-03-gap-list.json` is a **representative 93-record register** (not the complete Moderate gap set). Each record carries exactly one disposition:

- **`gap`** (81 records) — no sample mapping covers the control.
- **`strengthen`** (12 records) — a partial/contextual mapping exists but is insufficient on its own (e.g., AT-2 is partially addressed by CC2.x communication criteria; RA-3 by CC3.x risk assessment — both still need 800-53-grade evidence).

Every record: `control_id`, `gap_type`, `priority` (computed mix: High 8 / Medium 43 / Low 42), `owner`, `target_date_days`, `remediation_action`, and a per-engagement remediation-plan placeholder (no pre-baked evidence files are shipped).

Representative rows:

| Control | Disposition | Priority | Owner | Target | Remediation |
|---------|-------------|----------|-------|--------|-------------|
| RA-3 | strengthen | High | CISO | 30d | Risk assessment per 800-30 Rev 1; update annually |
| SI-4 | strengthen | High | CISO | 60d | Deploy SIEM with continuous monitoring; document in SSP §13 |
| SR-1 | gap | High | Procurement | 90d | Document SCRM policy |
| PT-7 | gap | Medium | Privacy Officer | 60d | Document PIA; agency approval |
| AT-2 | strengthen | Medium | HR / IT | 60d | Document quarterly security awareness training; track in LMS |
| IR-4(1) | gap | High | CISO | 60d | Automated IR playbook |

### Step 5 — Shared-responsibility / inheritance (Skill §4.6)

The SaaS runs on AWS. The inheritance map is:

- **AWS-inherited** (commercial, not FedRAMP): physical, environmental, hypervisor, base IAM — cite the provider's SOC 2 / ISO 27001 reports.
- **System-specific** (the SaaS implements): application-layer AC, AU, IA, SC, SI, IR.
- **Customer (agency)**: agency-side network controls, federated identity (SAML/OIDC), agency-side audit log review.

The customer responsibility matrix (CRM) is a deliverable. The crosswalk is the mapping; the CRM is the operating-model artifact.

## Variations / edge cases (test_adversarial.py)

- **V1 — PII volume increase:** if categorization moves to HIGH, the engagement re-baselines and the register grows (HIGH adds controls; it never removes records). The shipped invariant: register totals always foot.
- **V2 — PCI overlay changes the inheritance:** PCI scope reinforces some SOC 2 controls and surfaces new gaps; re-evaluate dispositions.
- **V3 — Customer wants FedRAMP:** the crosswalk may be insufficient; produce a SOC 2 → SOC 2 + 800-53 → FedRAMP roadmap.
- **V4 — Rev 5 privacy controls:** an agency requiring full Rev 5 privacy adds PT-family records to the register.
- **V5 — Sub-processor changes:** a sub-processor's SOC 2 status change moves controls between inherited and system-specific.
